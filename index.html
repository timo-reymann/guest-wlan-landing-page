What is the rainbow flag about?

It is more than just a colorful flag: Since the late 1970s, the rainbow flag has been a symbol of acceptance and equality for people who do not identify with the traditional role model of man and woman or other norms around gender and sexuality.
Lesbians, gays, bisexuals, non-binary and transgender people, for example, see themselves represented by the flag.
